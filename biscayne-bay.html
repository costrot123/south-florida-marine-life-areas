Biscayne Bay Marine Life

Biscayne Bay is home to Biscayne National Park where incredible mangrove forests and coral reefs can be found. 95 percent of Biscayne Bay is water therefore the majority of life that can be found here is marine. Manatees, different species of turtles, corals, and sea stars find refuge in the bay. With over 500 varieties of fish living in this area, snorkeling and diving are popular activities. For those who may not want to get in the water, boat tours are also available. An interesting fact about Biscayne Bay is that the American Crocodile can be found here, which can grow up to 19 feet long!

The Manatee

Video by: Calean Kelly, via Pixabay
Click the video to return to image.

Photo by Sam Farkas (NOAA Photo Library), Public domain, via Wikimedia Commons

The manatee, also called a sea cow, is a slow-moving mammal that can found in various locations throughout Florida. Biscayne’s bay mangrove forests make a great habitat for these large vegetarian creatures. Under ideal environments manatees grow to an average of 10 feet long! An interesting fact about manatees is that during the winter they seek warmer waters and therefore can be often found near powerplant wastewater which tends to be warm. The Star Fish

A variety of sea stars can be found inhabiting Biscayne bay. Despite also being referred to as star fish, sea stars are not fish. There are about 1,600 living species of sea stars spread across the world’s oceans. Biscayne bay is home to the Bahama Star and the Brittle Star. Mangrove Snapper

Photo by: Clinton & Charles Robertson from Del Rio, Texas & San Marcos, TX, USA, Via Wikimedia Commons used under: CC-BY-ND 2.0

The mangrove snapper, also known as the gray snapper, can be commonly found in Biscayne Bay and in other tropical waters. Snappers are valuable and are regarded as being delicious fish, therefore fishing for snappers is not uncommon. Although there are about 105 species of snappers, the gray snapper is the most prevelant in Biscayne Bay.
